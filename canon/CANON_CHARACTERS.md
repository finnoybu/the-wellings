# CANON_CHARACTERS.md

This document defines the canonical characters of *The Wellings* franchise.

Character definitions emphasize:
- emotional tone
- narrative function
- consistency of presence

They do not prescribe dialogue, actions, or internal thoughts
unless explicitly stated.

---

## THE WELLING FAMILY

### Arthur Welling (Father)

**Role:** Parent  
**Temperament:** Warm, steady, quietly thoughtful  

Arthur represents calm reliability without dominance.

**Canonical traits:**
- Never heroic or dramatic
- Never authoritarian
- Present and attentive
- Comfortable with pauses and uncertainty

Arthur’s presence often grounds scenes emotionally.
He may notice, wait, or assist, but does not lead through force or urgency.

Clothing and appearance should feel:
- practical
- timeless
- understated

Arthur is best described as an *accidental hero*:
someone who helps simply by being steady.

---

### Eleanor Welling (Mother)

**Role:** Parent  
**Temperament:** Calm strength, emotionally intelligent  

Eleanor provides emotional clarity and reassurance.

**Canonical traits:**
- Slightly formal, softly warm
- Expressive without exaggeration
- Emotionally perceptive
- Grounded and reassuring

Eleanor often:
- listens before responding
- names feelings gently
- brings quiet understanding into moments of uncertainty

Her tone is never dramatic or comedic.
Strength is shown through presence and calm awareness.

Eleanor may be referred to affectionately in dialogue
(e.g., shortened names) in later stories,
but her full name remains canonical.

---

### Oliver Welling (Child)

**Role:** Child / Primary viewpoint  
**Temperament:** Observant, gentle, curious  

Oliver is designed to be emotionally neutral enough
for many children to identify with.

**Canonical traits:**
- Curious without being precocious
- Gentle, not comedic
- Thoughtful and observant
- Often notices small details

Oliver frequently serves as the lens through which
the world is observed rather than explained.

He asks questions naturally,
without forcing lessons or conclusions.

Lines such as:
> “Oliver noticed something.”

are especially canonical in tone.

## ANIMAL COMPANION

### Miso (Pet)

**Role:** Companion  
**Temperament:** Quietly wise  

Miso is intentionally ambiguous.

**Canonical traits:**
- Species-neutral (cat-like, dog-like, or undefined)
- Calm and observant
- Never slapstick
- Never exaggerated or cartoonish

Miso may appear:
- watching
- waiting
- resting nearby

Miso’s role is atmospheric rather than active.
The character often reinforces stillness and presence.

---

## EXTENDED FAMILY

### Sofia Linden (Grandmother)

**Role:** Grandparent  
**Relation:** Eleanor’s mother  
**Temperament:** Warm, calm, emotionally thoughtful  

Sofia embodies gentle reassurance across generations.

**Canonical traits:**
- Slightly formal, but warmly affectionate
- Emotionally present
- Deeply loving toward her grandchild

Oliver refers to her affectionately as:
**“Gramma”**

Sofia’s presence often:
- slows moments
- brings perspective
- reinforces safety and continuity

Her husband (Mr. Linden) is **not yet canon**.
Her parents (Mr. and Mrs. Sanchez) are **not canon**.

These relationships must not be invented
unless explicitly introduced by the author.

---

## CANON CHARACTER RULES

- No additional family members may be introduced as canon
  without explicit author approval
- Character personalities must remain consistent across books and sets
- No character may become exaggerated, comedic, or dramatic
- Growth is allowed; personality shifts are not

Characters exist to support:
- observation
- warmth
- emotional continuity

They are not vehicles for lessons, jokes, or spectacle.
